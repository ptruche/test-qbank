A 13-year-old girl presents with abdominal fullness and a right adnexal mass on ultrasound. You recall that ovarian masses in children differ significantly in frequency and malignancy pattern compared with adults. Which of the following statements best reflects the epidemiology of ovarian neoplasms in children and adolescents?

<!-- EXPLANATION -->

### Teaching Summary

- Ovarian masses in children are uncommon (~5 per 100,000/yr), and about half are neoplastic; **~10–20% of neoplastic lesions are malignant**.  
- Under age 20, **germ cell tumors predominate (~75%)**; in adults, **epithelial tumors** predominate and often present advanced.  
- **Prepubertal (1–8 yrs)** patients have a higher malignant histology rate (~22%) than adolescents (~10%) despite lower overall incidence.  
- **Infant malignancy** is exceedingly rare.  

**Correct Answer: D — Germ cell tumors account for the majority of ovarian neoplasms in girls under 20.**

|---|---:|---|---|
| \<9 years | ~0.1 | Germ cell | Malignancy rare \<5 yrs |

### Clinical Pearls
- **Younger = germ cell first** (even if malignant).  
- **Epithelial tumors** → adult pattern; **rare** before puberty.  
- **Prepubertal**: lower incidence but **higher malignant proportion** than teens.
